This domain studies how small claims, low-value civil disputes, consumer disputes, neighborhood disputes, and simplified civil processes help or fail to restore resources without overwhelming subjects with procedural cost.

Human Infra value: low-dollar disputes can still break cash flow, housing, work tools, reputation, and trust when recovery channels are too slow, expensive, confusing, or inaccessible.

## Scope

- Small claims and simplified civil procedure at system level
- Consumer, landlord-tenant, property, contract, repair, and service disputes as examples
- Mediation, settlement conference, online dispute resolution, and court referral models
- Filing limits, fees, service, evidence, hearing access, judgment collection, and appeal boundaries at high level
- Self-help, plain-language, language access, disability access, and digital access interfaces

## Non-Goals

- No legal advice, demand letters, complaint drafting, evidence strategy, service instructions, settlement strategy, judgment collection advice, or appeal advice.
- No consumer-product recommendation, repair advice, debt collection strategy, harassment, retaliation, or business review manipulation.
- No prediction of dispute outcome or court jurisdiction.

## Source Spine

- National Center for State Courts civil justice and online dispute resolution materials.
- State court small-claims public guides as local examples.
- FTC and CFPB consumer dispute materials for consumer-protection context.
- Community mediation and access-to-justice literature for non-court alternatives.
