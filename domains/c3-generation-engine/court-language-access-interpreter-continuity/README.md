This domain studies whether limited-English-proficient participants can understand court notices, obtain qualified interpretation, receive translated materials, and participate meaningfully without losing rights through language barriers.

Human Infra value: court language access determines whether legal status, housing, debt, custody, safety, benefits, and records can be defended or understood by people who do not primarily use the court's dominant language.

## Scope

- Court interpreter access
- Language access plans
- Translated notices and plain-language materials
- LEP participant navigation
- Interpreter qualification and confidentiality at policy level
- Remote hearing language integration
- Civil, criminal, family, housing, and administrative court interfaces at high level

## Non-Goals

- No interpretation, translation, legal advice, form completion, complaint drafting, or case strategy.
- No certification, credentialing, or performance evaluation of individual interpreters.
- No collection of immigration status, ethnicity, nationality, address, case facts, or sensitive protected information.

## Source Spine

- DOJ language access and LEP materials.
- U.S. Courts interpreter services materials for federal context.
- National Center for State Courts language access resources.
- State court language access plans as implementation examples.
