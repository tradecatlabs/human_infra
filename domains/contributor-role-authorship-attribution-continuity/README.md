# Contributor Role, Authorship and Attribution Continuity

## Scope

This domain studies contributor roles, authorship attribution, CRediT taxonomy, acknowledgements, responsibility statements and credit continuity across papers, datasets, software and Web research artifacts.

Human Infra treats attribution as coordination infrastructure: when contributions are unclear, credit, accountability, maintenance ownership and collaboration incentives degrade.

## Human Infra Chain

```text
research work is collectively produced
  -> different people contribute concepts, data, code, analysis, writing and supervision
  -> contributor-role metadata makes contribution structure explicit
  -> credit, responsibility, maintenance and conflict review become easier to inspect
  -> collaboration networks become more trustworthy and durable
  -> subject capacity for cumulative research work increases
```

## Non-Goals

- No authorship dispute adjudication, legal advice or institutional policy interpretation.
- No ghost authorship, gift authorship, fake contribution or credit manipulation guidance.
- No collection of private authorship negotiations, emails or employment records.
- No claim that role labels alone prove integrity or responsibility.

## Source Signals

- CRediT taxonomy / NISO: https://credit.niso.org/
- ICMJE authorship recommendations: https://www.icmje.org/recommendations/
- ORCID: https://orcid.org/
- Crossref contributor metadata: https://www.crossref.org/documentation/
