# Court Fees, Fines, and Ability-to-Pay Continuity

This domain studies how court fees, fines, surcharges, payment plans, fee waivers, ability-to-pay review, collections, and license or warrant consequences affect subject continuity.

Human Infra value: court monetary obligations can convert a legal event into resource loss, debt spiral, license suspension, employment barriers, incarceration risk, and long-term exclusion when ability-to-pay safeguards fail.

## Scope

- Court fees, fines, costs, surcharges, and assessments
- Fee waivers and ability-to-pay review at system level
- Payment plans, collections, and nonpayment consequences
- License suspension, warrants, and failure-to-pay risk as policy issues
- Consumer debt and credit reporting intersections
- Equity, access-to-justice, and public finance tensions

## Non-Goals

- No legal advice, payment advice, debt advice, bankruptcy advice, fee-waiver application drafting, or case strategy.
- No instructions for avoiding payment, hiding assets, evading warrants, or manipulating income information.
- No prediction of what a court, clerk, probation office, collection agency, or DMV will do.

## Source Spine

- National Center for State Courts fines, fees, and access-to-justice materials.
- U.S. Courts fee schedules and public fee-waiver context for federal courts.
- DOJ and civil-rights materials on unconstitutional or discriminatory fines/fees practices where applicable.
- CFPB and consumer reporting materials for debt and collection intersections.
