# Self Represented Litigant Court Self-Help Continuity

This domain studies how people without counsel understand court process, locate neutral self-help resources, prepare required forms, track deadlines, and avoid procedural default.

Human Infra value: court access is a subject-continuity condition because housing, debt, family, benefits, safety, records, and consumer disputes can all fail when a person cannot understand or complete basic court tasks.

## Scope

- Self-represented litigant support
- Court self-help centers
- Legal information versus legal advice boundaries
- Form navigation and procedural literacy
- Deadline and status understanding
- Referral to legal aid, mediation, or specialist services
- Plain-language court materials

## Non-Goals

- No legal advice, litigation strategy, document drafting, form completion, deadline calculation, or case prediction.
- No replacement for attorneys, court staff, legal aid, interpreters, disability coordinators, or official local court rules.
- No guidance for evading service, misrepresenting facts, manipulating filings, or abusing court process.

## Source Spine

- National Center for State Courts access-to-justice and self-represented litigant materials.
- U.S. Courts public information on representing yourself.
- Legal Services Corporation justice gap and legal aid access materials.
- State and local court self-help portals for jurisdiction-specific examples.
