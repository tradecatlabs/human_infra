This domain studies whether people with disabilities can access court buildings, websites, hearings, filings, notices, jury service, and court programs with effective accommodations.

Human Infra value: legal rights are not continuous if court spaces, digital systems, communication channels, security screening, schedules, or hearing formats exclude people by mobility, sensory, cognitive, psychiatric, or chronic-health constraints.

## Scope

- Court disability accommodations
- ADA Title II and court program access at policy level
- Physical access, digital access, auxiliary aids, effective communication
- Accessible notices, forms, hearing formats, and jury service
- Remote hearing accessibility
- Coordinator, grievance, and complaint interfaces at high level

## Non-Goals

- No legal advice, accommodation request drafting, disability determination, medical documentation advice, or complaint strategy.
- No diagnosis, functional assessment, device recommendation, or accessibility certification.
- No exploitation of accessibility processes or false documentation.

## Source Spine

- ADA.gov state and local government accessibility materials.
- U.S. Courts and state court accessibility/accommodation resources.
- National Center for State Courts access and fairness resources.
- W3C WCAG and Section 508 materials for digital accessibility context.
