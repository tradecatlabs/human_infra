# 0080 Add Seventy-Sixth Wave Research Identity and Reproducibility Infrastructure Domains

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Human Infra is moving from qualitative theory collection toward evidence-bearing research pages, source cards, quantitative model contracts and reproducible Web artifacts. The existing open-science and trustworthy-communication domains cover the broad layer, but they do not yet isolate the specific infrastructure needed for durable research identity, persistent identifiers, repository-grade data sharing, executable analysis, preregistered plans, versioned protocols, contributor attribution, disclosure records and artifact preservation.

These are not cosmetic academic workflow topics. They are conditions of possibility for cumulative research: without them, claims cannot be linked to people, objects cannot be resolved, data cannot be rechecked, code cannot be rerun, plans cannot be compared with results, methods cannot be replicated, credit cannot be assigned and conflicts cannot be interpreted.

## Decision

Add the following domains:

- `scholarly-identity-orcid-affiliation-continuity/`
- `persistent-identifier-doi-citation-metadata-continuity/`
- `research-data-repository-fair-sharing-continuity/`
- `research-software-capsule-reproducibility-continuity/`
- `preregistration-registered-report-study-plan-continuity/`
- `lab-notebook-protocol-versioning-continuity/`
- `contributor-role-authorship-attribution-continuity/`
- `conflict-of-interest-funding-disclosure-continuity/`
- `research-artifact-archive-retention-preservation-continuity/`

## Boundary

This wave does not provide academic career advice, publication tactics, grant strategy, legal advice, authorship dispute resolution, wet-lab protocols, restricted-data access, confidential record handling, metric manipulation or misconduct accusation. It only defines research-infrastructure domains and evidence boundaries.

## Consequences

- Source-card and Web-paper work gains stronger infrastructure for identity, citation, data, code, protocols and disclosure.
- Quantitative Human Infra modeling gains clearer evidence provenance and reproducibility prerequisites.
- The domain map must distinguish broad open-science governance from these narrower artifact-level continuity conditions.
