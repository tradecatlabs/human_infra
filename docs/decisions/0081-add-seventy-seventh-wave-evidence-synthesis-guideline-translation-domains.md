# 0081 Add Seventy-Seventh Wave Evidence Synthesis and Guideline Translation Domains

## Status

Accepted

## Context

Human Infra now has domains for research identity, identifiers, data repositories, software reproducibility, preregistration, protocols, contributor roles, disclosure and artifact preservation. That makes evidence traceable, but traceable evidence is not yet synthesized evidence.

The next missing layer is how claims move from many studies into a usable model: reviews must have searchable protocols, studies need risk-of-bias appraisal, pooled estimates need heterogeneity analysis, reporting bias must be visible, certainty must be graded, guideline decisions must separate evidence from adoption, guidelines must be adapted to context, evidence must be updated, and urgent questions need rapid-review boundaries.

## Decision

Add the following domains:

- `systematic-review-protocol-search-screening-continuity/`
- `risk-of-bias-critical-appraisal-continuity/`
- `meta-analysis-effect-size-heterogeneity-continuity/`
- `publication-reporting-bias-detection-continuity/`
- `evidence-certainty-grade-summary-findings-continuity/`
- `evidence-to-decision-guideline-panel-continuity/`
- `guideline-appraisal-adaptation-localization-continuity/`
- `living-evidence-surveillance-update-continuity/`
- `rapid-review-emergency-evidence-triage-continuity/`

## Boundary

This wave does not provide clinical recommendations, treatment rankings, policy mandates, emergency commands, statistical consulting, regulatory submissions, publication tactics, accusation handling or guideline-writing services. It defines evidence-synthesis and guideline-translation domains so Human Infra can downgrade, update and contextualize claims before they enter models or public narratives.

## Consequences

- Source cards can distinguish raw study relevance from risk of bias, certainty, synthesis quality, reporting bias and guideline transferability.
- Quantitative model pages gain better gates for effect-size admission, uncertainty, heterogeneity and stale evidence.
- Future Web papers can label provisional, low-certainty, living and guideline-derived claims without pretending they are settled facts.
