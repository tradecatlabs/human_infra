# 0049 Add Forty-Fifth-Wave Legal Status Coercive Process Continuity Domains

- Date: 2026-06-30
- Status: Accepted

## Context

The previous wave added asset, account, mobility, and transaction-execution continuity domains. A further transcendental pass shows another legal substrate beneath those resources: subjects must remain recognized through nationality, immigration status, protection status, confinement status, civil commitment boundaries, court-notice procedure, and criminal-record relief. These are not generic legal-advice topics; they are institutional state variables that can either preserve or sever agency, mobility, income, family continuity, and future option value.

## Decision

Add seven formal research domains:

- `domains/c6-local-unlocking/citizenship-nationality-statelessness-continuity/`
- `domains/c6-local-unlocking/immigration-status-residency-work-authorization-continuity/`
- `domains/c5-ecological-substrate/asylum-refugee-protection-status-continuity/`
- `domains/c6-local-unlocking/detention-custody-confinement-status-continuity/`
- `domains/c6-local-unlocking/civil-commitment-involuntary-treatment-rights-continuity/`
- `domains/c5-ecological-substrate/court-notice-service-process-default-judgment-continuity/`
- `domains/c6-local-unlocking/criminal-record-relief-collateral-consequences-continuity/`

Each domain starts with only `README.md` and `AGENTS.md`. No empty data, scripts, or model directories are created.

## Consequences

- The domain map can now distinguish generic legal identity, humanitarian migration, and access-to-justice from more specific legal-state continuity variables.
- These domains must not become personal citizenship, immigration, asylum, detention, mental-health commitment, court, expungement, criminal, or legal-advice services.
- Source priority is official or high-authority public material: USCIS, U.S. Department of State, UNHCR, ICE, BOP, BJS, SAMHSA, HHS OCR, DOJ Civil Rights, U.S. Courts, NCSC, NICCC, BJA, and equivalent jurisdictional official sources.
